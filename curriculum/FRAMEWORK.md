## Design Philosophy

LUMINA's curriculum is not a traditional school curriculum. It is designed for **children whose education has been interrupted** — sometimes for months, sometimes for years. It meets children at their actual level, not their age-expected level, and advances at their pace.

The framework is built on three commitments:

1. **Relevance** — Content connects to children's lived experience, including displacement and crisis
2. **Flexibility** — Non-linear, child-directed pathways with no grade-level shaming
3. **Humanity** — Learning is always secondary to wellbeing. A child who is not ready to learn is never pushed.

## Age Groups

| Group | Age Range | Focus |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Seedlings | 5–7 | Pre-literacy, number sense, play-based discovery |
| Sprouts | 8–10 | Foundational literacy & numeracy, storytelling |
| Growers | 11–13 | Intermediate skills, critical thinking, self-expression |
| Bridges | 14–17 | Advanced literacy, practical life skills, vocational awareness |

Age groups are **advisory**, not prescriptive. ARIA's dynamic assessment overrides age-group assumptions.

## Curriculum Domains

### Domain 1: Literacy
**Scope:** Letter recognition → phonics → decoding → reading comprehension → creative writing

**Approach:**
- Stories drawn from culturally familiar contexts
- Oral tradition respected and centered (storytelling before reading)
- Images and audio support for pre-literate children
- Children are encouraged to create their own stories

**Milestone markers (non-tested, observational):**
- Can identify letters in their language
- Can decode simple words
- Can read a short story with support
- Can read independently
- Can write a simple sentence
- Can write a short story

### Domain 2: Numeracy
**Scope:** Number recognition → counting → basic operations → fractions → practical math

**Approach:**
- Grounded in real-world scenarios relevant to children's lives (market math, sharing, measuring)
- Visual and tactile approaches (counting games, spatial reasoning)
- No abstract mathematics until concrete foundations are solid

### Domain 3: Life Skills
**Scope:** Personal safety, hygiene, nutrition, emotional literacy, rights awareness

**Special modules:**
- **My Body, My Rights** — age-appropriate body safety and consent education
- **Staying Safe** — recognizing dangerous situations, trusted adults, what to do
- **Feelings & Me** — emotional vocabulary, regulation basics
- **Staying Healthy** — hygiene, nutrition basics relevant to crisis context
- **My Rights** — child-appropriate introduction to rights (UNCRC)

### Domain 4: Creative Expression
**Scope:** Storytelling, visual art prompts, music, imaginative play

**Purpose:** Creative expression is not supplementary — it is therapeutic. For children processing trauma, creative modalities provide a safe outlet that words often cannot.

**Activities:**
- Story completion (ARIA provides opening, child continues)
- Draw and describe (child draws on device, ARIA responds to their description)
- Create-a-world (imaginative world-building games)
- My Story (guided narrative journaling — private, never read by LUMINA)

## Localization Framework

All curriculum content is designed for **localization**, not just translation. Localization includes:

- **Language** — Full translation via Gemma 4's 140+ language support
- **Cultural context** — Names, settings, and examples adapted to regional culture
- **Visual representation** — Character art reflecting regional diversity
- **Religious sensitivity** — Content reviewed for religious neutrality; adaptable for faith contexts
- **Trauma context** — Regional crisis types reflected in safety modules (e.g., flood safety vs. conflict safety)

### Priority Localization Regions (Phase 1)
1. Sub-Saharan Africa (Swahili, Hausa, Amharic, French, Arabic)
2. Middle East & North Africa (Arabic, Dari, Pashto, Kurmanji Kurdish)
3. South & Southeast Asia (Bengali, Rohingya, Burmese, Hindi, Tagalog)
4. Eastern Europe (Ukrainian, Russian)
5. Central America (Spanish, indigenous languages: Q'eqchi', Mam, K'iche')

## Assessment Philosophy

LUMINA does **not test children**. There are no grades, no scores, no pass/fail.

ARIA assesses learning level through **naturalistic observation** — analyzing response patterns, vocabulary used, problem-solving approaches — without the child ever knowing they are being assessed.

Progress is tracked locally and presented to children and caregivers as a **growth story**, not a score. "Look how far you've come" not "Here is your grade."

## Content Loading

Curriculum content is pre-loaded on the device during initial setup. Packages are organized by:
- Language
- Age group
- Domain
- Connectivity tier (full / minimal)

Content updates are delivered via opportunistic sync when NGO connectivity is available.
