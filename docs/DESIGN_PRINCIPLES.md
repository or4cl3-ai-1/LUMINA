## Why Trauma-Informed Design?

Children in humanitarian crises are not typical users. They carry the weight of displacement, loss, violence, and uncertainty. Standard educational software — designed for children in stable, connected, resource-rich environments — fails them completely.

LUMINA is designed from the ground up around the six core principles of **Trauma-Informed Care (TIC)**, adapted for AI interaction design:

---

## The Six Principles

### 1. Safety
*The child must feel physically and emotionally safe before any learning can begin.*

**In LUMINA:**
- Every session opens with HAVEN — not ARIA. Learning never begins cold.
- The UI uses warm, non-clinical colors and familiar, culturally appropriate visual language
- No time pressure, no countdowns, no failure sounds
- The child can end any interaction at any time without explanation
- HAVEN explicitly establishes: "You are safe here. We go at your pace."

### 2. Trustworthiness & Transparency
*The child must understand what LUMINA is and what it does.*

**In LUMINA:**
- Age-appropriate explanation of what LUMINA is on first use
- No hidden data collection — the app is honest about what it stores
- Consistent, predictable behavior — no surprise interactions
- HAVEN never pretends to be human

### 3. Peer Support
*Connection with others who share similar experiences reduces isolation.*

**In LUMINA:**
- Optional teacher/facilitator mode enables group learning contexts
- Shared progress boards (opt-in, anonymized) for classroom cohesion
- Stories and examples in curriculum feature characters with experiences similar to the child's

### 4. Collaboration & Mutuality
*Power differentials should be minimized. The child has agency.*

**In LUMINA:**
- Children choose what to learn and when
- ARIA asks permission before moving to a new topic
- Children can name their LUMINA companion (personalizing ARIA and HAVEN)
- Children can provide feedback on activities — LUMINA listens and adjusts

### 5. Empowerment, Voice & Choice
*Trauma often involves loss of control. LUMINA restores it.*

**In LUMINA:**
- Every activity presents choices — not a single forced path
- Children are celebrated for effort, not just correctness
- HAVEN explicitly names and validates emotions without directing them
- Children can journal privately — entries are never read by LUMINA agents

### 6. Cultural, Historical & Gender Sensitivity
*Trauma does not exist outside of cultural context.*

**In LUMINA:**
- All curriculum content is culturally reviewed and regionally adaptable
- Gemma 4's 140+ language support extends to dialects and regional variants
- No gender assumptions in any interaction or curriculum content
- Historical and cultural trauma (displacement, colonization, ethnic persecution) is never minimized
- Religious and spiritual coping mechanisms are respected and never challenged

## Additional Design Principles

### Radical Simplicity
The UI must be usable by a child who has never touched a smartphone. Icon-forward, minimal text in navigation, large touch targets, forgiving interaction design.

### Graceful Degradation
If the model performs slowly on low-end hardware, LUMINA degrades gracefully — simpler responses, fewer tokens, but never a broken experience.

### Emotional Pacing
Learning sessions have a maximum emotional load. If HAVEN detects elevated stress over a sustained period, ARIA pauses and HAVEN leads a grounding activity before resuming.

### The Companion Model
LUMINA's agents are not tools. They are companions. HAVEN and ARIA have consistent personalities — warm, patient, curious, encouraging — that persist across sessions, creating continuity of relationship for children who may have very little stability in their lives.

## Research Foundation

LUMINA's trauma-informed design draws from:

- SAMHSA's Six Principles of Trauma-Informed Care (2014)
- UNICEF's Minimum Standards for Education in Emergencies (INEE, 2010)
- Sphere Handbook — Humanitarian Charter and Minimum Standards (2018)
- WHO Mental Health Gap Action Programme (mhGAP)
- Save the Children: "Healing and Education Through the Arts" (HEART) framework
- Inter-Agency Network for Education in Emergencies (INEE) guidance
